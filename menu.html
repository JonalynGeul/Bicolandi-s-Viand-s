Bicol Region

is best known for its breathtaking. natural wonders and tourist spots like the perfect cone-shaped Mount Mayon in Albay. But aside from its beautiful beaches, Bicol is also known for its distinct cuisine, which mostly features the laing or pinangat, sinantol, kinunot and tinumok. Every year, the Bicolanos celebrate the month-long Bicol Food Festival in September. So, its the perfect time to explore all of the best food offerings from the region.

Menu

  • Laing

    or Pinangat, another popular Bicolano delicacy.

    This dish is made with laing or taro leaves,coconut cream,siling labuyo,shrimp paste,lemon grass,garlic.

  • Kinunot

    means "to tear a part" in Bicolano.

    It usually made with flaked fish,specifically shark or stingray meat.

  • Sinantol

    or Ginataang Santol.

    is made of sheredded santol, sili and gata along with either smoked fish or pork.

  • Tilmok

    made with crab meat and coconut.

    By combining and steaming crab meat and coconut meat and wrapped in banana leaves.

  • Kandinga

    made up of diced pork or beef

    lungs,spleen and heart.This can be served as an appetizer for beer and alcoholic beverages.
